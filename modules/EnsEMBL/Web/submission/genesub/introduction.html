Gene Submission
  

Welcome to the Mosquito Gene submission pages

The gene submission page allows biologists to request gene name assignments for genes they have studied. It also allows detailed knowledge of the gene structure to be conveyed to Ensembl - Ensembl is planning to update the mosquito database every 6 months and during the update we will do everything possible to recapitulate the proposed gene structure.

The gene name assignments are human readable symbols suitable for publication. Submission of a gene name through this form does not mean it has been accepted, as there are additional checks which must be performed before we assign the symbol. We are following guidelines agreed by the mosquito community and are outlined in the Guidelines section. Researchers will be notified when a gene symbol is assigned, if the gene you wish to submit has been experimentally verified and has been published (or will be published) we also invite you to submit it to the EMBL database.

Ideally gene structures will be accompanied by direct cDNA sequencing evidence or other experimental evidence. When submitting your gene name you can identify other EMBL/Genbank sequences which correspond to your gene (please, do not indicate the genomic sequence here).

Please provide conceptual cDNA of the gene, even if you have built a gene structure without experimental data. The conceptual cDNA is by far the most robust way of indicating what you believe the gene structure is, as it will be relevant even after updates to the genomic sequence.

We will warn you if you are submitting a cDNA with an open reading frame less than 50 amino acids as it is likely that this is an error when constructing the cDNA (however, small peptides and/or RNA genes do exist, so they will be accepted)

For more information about this area please contact our helpdesk.
